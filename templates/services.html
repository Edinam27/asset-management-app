Our Services

Fixed Asset Management Services

1. Asset Tracking

Scope: Asset tracking involves the continuous monitoring and recording of physical assets. This activity ensures that all assets are accounted for, their location is known, and their usage and condition are tracked over time.

Process:

  • Identification: Each asset is given a unique identifier, such as a barcode, RFID tag, or serial number.
  • Registration: Assets are recorded in an asset management system, capturing details like acquisition date, cost, location, and condition.
  • Movement Tracking: Every movement of the asset, whether it's a change in location or a transfer of custody, is recorded.
  • Condition Monitoring: Regular checks are conducted to assess the condition of assets, noting any wear and tear or damage.
  • Audits: Periodic physical audits are performed to reconcile system records with actual assets, ensuring accuracy and accountability.

2. Maintenance Scheduling

Scope: Maintenance scheduling involves planning and executing regular maintenance activities to ensure assets remain in good working condition and to extend their useful life.

Process:

  • Preventive Maintenance Planning: Create a schedule for routine maintenance tasks based on manufacturer recommendations, usage data, and historical maintenance records.
  • Predictive Maintenance: Use data analytics and IoT sensors to predict when maintenance should be performed based on the actual condition of the asset rather than a fixed schedule.
  • Work Order Management: Generate and manage work orders for maintenance activities, including assigning tasks to technicians and tracking completion.
  • Record Keeping: Document all maintenance activities, including parts used, labor hours, and any issues encountered.
  • Compliance: Ensure maintenance activities comply with industry standards and regulatory requirements.

3. Reporting and Analytics

Scope: Reporting and analytics involve collecting, analyzing, and presenting data on asset performance, utilization, and lifecycle costs to inform decision-making and improve asset management strategies.

Process:

  • Data Collection: Gather data from various sources, including asset tracking systems, maintenance records, and financial systems.
  • Data Integration: Integrate data into a centralized system for comprehensive analysis.
  • KPI Development: Develop key performance indicators (KPIs) to measure asset performance, such as uptime, maintenance costs, and return on assets.
  • Analysis: Use analytical tools and techniques to identify trends, patterns, and insights related to asset utilization, performance, and costs.
  • Reporting: Generate reports and dashboards that provide actionable insights for stakeholders, including executives, operations managers, and maintenance teams.
  • Continuous Improvement: Use the insights gained from analytics to refine asset management strategies, improve operational efficiency, and reduce costs.

4. Customizable Asset Categories
